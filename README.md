# Casebriefr

**A professional legal case briefing tool for law students and legal professionals.**

---

## Overview

Casebriefr is a clean, printable HTML template for creating structured legal case briefs. It guides you through the essential components of case analysis with helpful prompts and a professional layout.

## Features

- ✅ **Structured Sections** — All essential case brief components:
  - Case Identification (name, citation, court, date, judge)
  - Facts
  - Procedural History
  - Issue(s)
  - Holding
  - Rule of Law
  - Reasoning/Rationale
  - Concurring & Dissenting Opinions
  - Analysis & Significance

- ✅ **Print-Optimized** — Clean formatting that looks great on paper or as PDF
- ✅ **Fillable Fields** — Type directly in your browser, then print or save as PDF
- ✅ **Helpful Hints** — Guidance for each section to ensure comprehensive briefs
- ✅ **Professional Design** — Clean, readable layout suitable for class or court

## How to Use

1. Open `index.html` in any web browser
2. Fill in the case information in each section
3. Click "Print / Save as PDF" when finished
4. Save digitally or print for your records

## Sections Explained

### Case Identification
Basic bibliographic information about the case.

### Facts
Summarize the legally relevant facts that led to the dispute. Focus on facts material to the court's decision.

### Procedural History
Trace the case's path through the judicial system to the current court.

### Issue(s)
Frame the legal question(s) the court must answer. Write as yes/no questions.

### Holding
The court's answer to the legal question(s) — the outcome of the case.

### Rule of Law
The legal principle, test, or standard the court applied or established.

### Reasoning
The court's step-by-step analysis and logic.

### Concurring & Dissenting Opinions
Alternative perspectives from other justices/judges.

### Analysis & Significance
Why this case matters and its broader impact.
